The whole idea in one paragraph

Mental model

This is a private operating system for outreach, not an autonomous email cannon. It gives agents enough memory and structure to research, draft, compare, and prioritize intelligently, while keeping every externally visible action under human control.

The local system remembers why a prospect matters, which evidence supports each sentence, how a draft changed, what was approved, what Instantly actually sent, what came back, and what should happen next. The point is to make high-personalization outreach faster and more cumulative without lowering the reputational bar.

Why build this at all?

The project exists because the current workflow loses context at exactly the moments where judgment matters.

Problem 1

Each campaign starts too fresh

  • Research, draft history, approvals, and outcomes live in different places.
  • A new agent cannot reliably reconstruct why an old email worked or failed.
  • Useful connection and proof mechanisms are rediscovered instead of reused deliberately.
Problem 2

The market is reputation-sensitive

  • Many recipients are peers, economists, academics, or policy professionals.
  • A weak factual hook or awkward named connection can cost more than one missed reply.
  • Bulk automation is therefore the wrong default even if it is technically possible.
Problem 3

Replies are perishable

  • A promising response should arrive with its original research and campaign context attached.
  • The agent should prepare a next step quickly, but never send the reply by itself.
  • Opt-outs must become permanent suppression before any later outreach can proceed.
Opportunity

The work can compound

  • The system can learn which public-work hooks, proof routes, CTAs, and connection paths work.
  • It can borrow mechanisms without copying private correspondence across prospects.
  • The result is better human judgment with less reconstruction work.

The operating loop

Think of the system as a loop with human checkpoints, not a funnel that becomes autonomous after a draft is generated.

1
Research
Collect public evidence, buyer context, connections, and uncertainty.
2
Draft
Create a versioned message and explain the mechanism behind it.
3
Approve
A human freezes one exact version. Nothing is published yet.
4
Publish paused
Copy approved intent into a paused Instantly campaign and verify it.
5
Launch
Run fresh checks and separately authorize delivery.
6
Observe and learn
Import delivery, replies, outcomes, and lessons back into the corpus.

There is no single database that is true about everything

The design works by assigning different kinds of truth to the system that is actually qualified to own them.

Owns

Instantly

What the delivery system observed

Owns

Owns

Boundary
The outreach system proposes updates; it does not silently rewrite relationship records

What local-first means here

Not merely storage location

The working memory lives in a private local SQLite corpus. A single local service is the only writer, so the UI, CLI, agents, and background automation cannot invent different versions of reality.

Local-first also means the system can be useful when it is not hosted continuously. But it does not pretend the Mac is always online: Instantly-native limits and stop behavior remain necessary because local automation cannot be the emergency brake while the computer is asleep.

  • Private prospect data and messages stay outside Git.
  • Phone access is private through Tailscale, never a public web app.
  • Encrypted attachments and backups remain recoverable without moving the primary corpus to a cloud database.

Approval, publishing, and launch are three different decisions

This distinction is the central safety property. Collapsing any two of these would make review less meaningful.

Decision What it means What it does not mean Required evidence
Approve I endorse this exact local version for this prospect No Instantly campaign or lead is created Exact copy, evidence, compliance, suppression, relationship, and reviewed remote-state fingerprints
Publish Copy the approved bundle into the confirmed paused campaign Nothing is allowed to deliver Fresh sync, fresh suppression and relationship checks, paused-state readback, exact remote mapping
Launch Permit the bounded confirmed campaign to deliver No permission to change copy, recipients, accounts, or limits A second fresh preflight, hard remote caps, worst-case send count, and explicit human confirmation

Follow one prospect through the system

  1. Create the prospect record

    Store identity, organization, role, public sources, buyer context, and why this person belongs in the campaign.

  2. Assemble personalization evidence

    Record a public-work hook, possible connection path, proof route, uncertainty, and whether each fact has been verified.

  3. Generate a message version

    The agent writes a draft, labels the mechanism it used, cites the evidence, and explains its recommendation.

  4. Prioritize the review

    The queue balances opportunity value, reputational risk, factual uncertainty, named connections, reply urgency, and relationship potential.

  5. Review one person

    You approve, request a revision, skip, reject, or override the suggested review tier. Every outcome is durable.

  6. Freeze the approval

    Approval binds one exact version and its evidence. A clean later sync refreshes eligibility; a meaningful change invalidates it.

  7. Publish into a paused campaign

    The service writes a durable intent, calls Instantly, and reads the result back lead by lead.

  8. Launch separately

    The service shows the exact bounded blast radius and asks for a new human confirmation.

  9. Import what happened

    Delivery, replies, attachments, and campaign events return as append-only observations rather than overwriting intent.

  10. Record the outcome

    The agent can now compare the original strategy with what actually happened and reuse the mechanism responsibly later.

The subtle idea: ingestion and permission to send move separately

A failed suppression write must not make us lose later replies, but it must stop later sending. The plan therefore uses two watermarks.

Observe
Ingestion watermark
How far we have durably captured remote messages and events.
Obligate
Suppression ledger
Every observed opt-out becomes a durable obligation before anything can forget it.
Verify
DNC receipt
The permanent suppression store independently confirms the address is blocked.
Permit
Eligibility watermark
Only advances after every suppression obligation through that point is verified.

Four failure modes the architecture is designed around

Drift

Local intent and Instantly disagree

  • Preserve both versions.
  • Identify the affected prospect or campaign.
  • Block only the bounded affected scope when possible.
  • Require human reconciliation before continuing.
Ambiguity

The API response is lost

  • Do not assume the mutation failed.
  • Read remote state before considering a retry.
  • Keep the result unknown when absence cannot be proven.
  • Never automatically retry an ambiguous reply-send.
Suppression

An opt-out lands between two databases

  • Create a durable obligation first.
  • Verify the permanent DNC write separately.
  • Reconcile interrupted transitions after restart.
  • Keep remote writes blocked until the receipt exists.
Recovery

A valid old backup could be unsafe

  • Restore into quarantine with remote credentials disabled.
  • Never roll permanent suppression backward.
  • Quarantine unfinished mutation intents from replay.
  • Rebuild search only after canonical history passes integrity checks.

What happens when someone replies

  1. Poll and catch up

    The local watcher resumes from its last checkpoint whenever the environment is available.

  2. Capture before judging

    Store the exact available message and provenance; fetch safe attachment bytes while their links still work.

  3. Handle opt-outs first

    A deterministic opt-out is written to permanent suppression before the thread can become send-eligible again.

  4. Classify with uncertainty

    Label automatic, opt-out, substantive, referral, positive, negative, or unclear, and retain confidence plus evidence.

  5. Reconstruct the context

    Bring together the thread, original evidence, campaign strategy, prospect history, and analogous prior outcomes.

  6. Draft but do not send

    The agent proposes a response and next action. Every real reply still receives individual human approval.

  7. Propose relationship admission

    A substantive interaction produces a curated proposal for the existing relationship workflow, not an automatic CRM write.

Who can do what?

Agents get broad ability to think and prepare. Humans keep authority over externally visible or boundary-changing actions.

Capability Human Interactive agent Automation
Research, search, compare, draft Yes Yes, within a scoped audited session No broad exploration
Read-only sync and status Yes Yes Yes
Classify and propose next actions Yes Yes Yes, as proposals
Record a verified opt-out Reviewable Observe result May trigger the deterministic safety path
Approve exact copy Yes No No
Publish, launch, or reply-send Yes, with a fresh action-bound confirmation No No
Resolve drift or confidentiality exceptions Yes Propose only No
Promote a restore or apply a relationship update Yes, through the owning workflow No No

The review experience on desktop and phone

Desktop

Queue plus focused detail

  • The left side explains what needs attention and why it is prioritized.
  • The detail view keeps exact copy, evidence, history, blockers, and actions together.
  • Reply urgency can outrank draft review without hiding the underlying ranking logic.
Phone

One prospect at a time

  • A stable sequential flow replaces a compressed desktop dashboard.
  • Next and previous navigation preserves place after each decision.
  • The action region is touch-safe, and previews clearly return to the underlying record.
Both

Same facts, different layout

  • UI and agent tools consume the same canonical work-item description.
  • Disabled actions explain the actual blocker instead of merely disappearing.
  • A stale record is refreshed without losing the attempted operator input.
Private access

Tailscale, not public hosting

  • The service listens only on the local machine.
  • A new device pairs with a short-lived code displayed on the Mac.
  • Public, LAN, forged-identity, expired-session, and CSRF cases are tested negatively.

The rollout ladder

No gate inherits authority from the previous one. Each gate enables one bounded class of action and can be revoked independently.

Gate What becomes possible What must be proven first
G0 - Fixtures Local corpus, review UI, agent tools, fake adapters Automated tests, private paths, database integrity
G1 - Disposable read Read-only sync of a dedicated test campaign Exact bodies, threads, directions, cursors, attachments, and remote states
G2 - Disposable publish Paused publish to at most two self-controlled recipients Zero scheduled delivery, exact readback, per-lead mapping, lost-response reconciliation
G3 - Self-controlled send Activation, delivery, reply-send/readback, and pause Exactly-once bounded behavior, settlement, no unexpected delivery
G4 - Academic-econ pilot One bounded external tranche Campaign QA, suppression, proof routing, jurisdiction pack, sender health, hard caps, recovery, iPhone access, and explicit approval

How the build is sequenced

  1. U1 - Build the private foundation

    Create the local package, service ownership, migration framework, private paths, and doctor checks.

  2. U2 - Make research and drafts durable

    Add prospect evidence, immutable versions, retrieval, agent sessions, and shared UI/CLI work-item contracts.

  3. U3 - Add suppression and exact approval

    Connect the permanent DNC authority, relationship checks, review priority, and approval binding.

  4. U4 - Mirror Instantly safely

    Implement read-only sync, separate ingestion and eligibility watermarks, attachments, and drift.

  5. U5 and U6 - Add remote actions and reply operations

    Build the mutation outbox, publish/launch controls, reply triage, reply-send, and relationship proposals.

  6. U7 - Validate the review experience early, then connect it

    Start with fixture-backed desktop/phone review after U3; integrate live state and private phone access later.

  7. U8 - Prove recovery before real data

    Create sealed encrypted snapshots and restore into quarantine without rolling suppression backward.

  8. U9 - Execute the gates and open a bounded pilot

    Run the disposable proofs, verify operational readiness, complete a self-controlled rehearsal, and only then open G4.

What this deliberately does not become in v1

Not autonomous

No autonomous cold email or replies

  • Agents may prepare and recommend.
  • Humans approve every externally visible message state.
Not a delivery engine

No replacement for Instantly

  • Instantly still sends and owns observed delivery state.
  • The control plane adds memory, judgment, and reconciliation.
Not a general CRM

No competing relationship database

  • Substantive replies create proposals.
  • The existing relationship workflow remains authoritative.
Not cloud coordination

No Turso or direct mailbox sync yet

  • SQLite remains the primary corpus.
  • Google and Microsoft mailbox reconciliation stays deferred.

Where we are right now

Planning complete

The conceptual and implementation plan is complete. It has been confidence-deepened, reviewed through architecture, data-integrity, agent, product, design, security, feasibility, scope, and adversarial lenses, and converted into nine implementation milestones.

No control-plane application has been built yet. The next substantive choice is execution style: a supervised build-and-review workflow or a longer autonomous goal run. In either case, the plan says to start with local fixtures and private foundations, not with real recipients or live sending.

Glossary and durable principles

Use these definitions when reading the engineering plan or evaluating implementation work.

Key terms

  • Corpus: the private local operational memory.
  • Checkpoint: a durable record of completed synchronization work.
  • Ingestion watermark: how far remote observations have been safely captured.
  • Eligibility watermark: how far the system may rely on suppression and safety obligations being complete.
  • Drift: a disagreement between approved local intent and observed remote state.
  • Mutation outbox: durable local intent recorded before an external write.
  • Action capability: a short-lived, one-use human confirmation bound to exact inputs.
  • Sealed epoch: a fully copied, verified, encrypted backup generation.

Principles that should survive implementation details

  • Preserve intent and observation separately.
  • Do not infer remote failure from a lost response.
  • Suppression becomes permanent before later sending becomes eligible.
  • Approval never implies publishing; publishing never implies launch.
  • Private correspondence may inform mechanisms, but may not be transplanted across prospects without approval.
  • Recovery must not roll suppression backward or replay unfinished external actions.
  • The default unit of judgment is one person, not one batch.
