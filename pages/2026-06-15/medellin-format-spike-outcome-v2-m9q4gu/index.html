Executive Summary

Recommendation

Edition #1 should ship as a compact weekly briefing, not an event newsletter with extras. The launch shape should be: one-time reintroduction, This Week's Signal, The Shortlist, Move Smarter, and Personal Note.

The key change is that events stay important but stop being the whole product. The email carries judgment: what changed, what is worth doing, and how to move through Medellin better.

  • Persistent blocks after Edition #1: This Week's Signal, The Shortlist, Move Smarter, Personal Note.
  • Experiments for Editions #2-#4: social carousel, Local Moment visual post, participatory prompt or Builder Profile lead.
  • Deferred: standing real estate coverage, full Format Radar automation, regular Facebook-group extraction.

Recommended Edition Shape

The Edition should read like a guided briefing. Instagram and app/web get derivatives and durable references; email keeps the complete operator read.

Four-Edition Experiment Plan

  1. Edition #1: prove the core briefing

    Ship reintroduction, This Week's Signal, Shortlist, Move Smarter, and Personal Note. Success is operator approval in about one hour and no block feeling like filler.

  2. Edition #2: social carousel derivative

    Turn one Shortlist or Signal item into a saveable Instagram carousel. Stop if the carousel duplicates all email value or becomes manual design work.

  3. Edition #3: Local Moment visual post

    Test real stills or short clips, with generated motion only if it looks natural. Stop if it feels synthetic, tourist-board-like, or labor-intensive.

  4. Edition #4: participatory prompt

    Ask for reader nominations, a tiny quest, or Builder Profile leads. Stop if it attracts low-quality promotion or distracts from cadence.

Surface Routing

Email keeps the relationship

Email owns the complete Edition: operator interpretation, criteria, practical caveats, source context, and the signed Personal Note.

Instagram gets unsigned Brand Voice derivatives: Shortlist carousel, Signal explainer, Local Moment, Move Smarter teaser, and subscribe CTA.

App and web own breadth: the full event database, saved events, durable guides, venue/neighborhood pages, and future reader-nominated references.

Appendix: Handoff Summary

The full repo docs remain the source of truth. This published report is a review surface.

Decision memo

The decision memo recommends the locked launch order and challenges the old five-events / one-Nugget / one-note assumption.

  • Reframe five events to a 3-5 item Shortlist.
  • Rename the reader-facing Nugget lane to Move Smarter.
  • Keep the Personal Note short, signed, and email-exclusive.

Revised requirements

The implementation handoff keeps the Edition as a git-versioned artifact and avoids new root Convex surfaces for this decision.

  • No new admin GUI or database table is implied by the format decision.
  • Private Research Signals and Evidence Pointers stay out of public outputs.
  • The next product task is adapting the Edition file/template/rendering path to this block order.
