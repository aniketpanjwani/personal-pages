Executive Summary

What changed in v3

This v4 report keeps the richer source-dossier structure from v3 and adds one important correction: the Medellingles Time Out food-cities carousel should be treated as likely coordinated/source-owned, not as a pure same-day monitoring benchmark. The carousel caption discloses that Time Out asked Medellingles to write the Medellin portion, and Time Out lists Luis Gomez as its Medellin contributor.

The launch recommendation stays stable: Edition #1 should be a compact weekly briefing with judgment, not an events-only newsletter. But the source pages now show clearer reusable mechanics: Medellingles for source-to-carousel adaptation, Winnipeg Digest for utility routing, Catskill Crew for voice/community depth, Remote Roberto and Medellin Tour Guidee for newcomer pain-point hooks, and Naptown Scoop for local-moment video packaging.

  • Use Medellingles as the closest tactical model for source-to-carousel adaptation, but discount coordinated contributor examples when estimating automation speed.
  • Treat RotoWire as the cleanest same-day external-source benchmark; treat GPI as roughly one day from launch or five days from resource publication.
  • Use Winnipeg Digest and Catskill Crew to keep the email useful and readable rather than merely timely.
  • Use creator accounts for hooks and anxieties, but translate their service/funnel language into Medellin Insider's trustworthy editorial voice.

Medellin Insider adaptation

Ideas To Steal

  • Create a weekly Medellin Intelligence block: one outside source, one local translation, one why-it-matters note, one link for deeper reading.
  • Turn source-backed items into carousels: headline, local implication, caveat, source credit, save/share prompt.
  • Maintain app/web reference pages for durable utilities: direct flights, restaurant updates, recurring events, holiday/festival guides, and safety/civic explainers.
  • Use Instagram as a research and distribution surface, but keep the Edition as the place where operator judgment and source links accumulate.
Taste boundary

What Not To Copy

  • Do not become a static paid directory or generic calendar; Insider needs a sharper operator voice and a reason to open weekly.
  • Do not over-index on diaspora World Cup content unless it maps back to the Medellin Insider audience or a current local moment.
  • Do not treat likes as sufficient proof of launch fit; some high-performing identity posts may be better for Instagram than email.

Voice, trust, and community mechanics

Source Dossier: Catskill Crew

Catskill Crew is the strongest writing/product-quality anchor. It proves a local newsletter can be enjoyable to read while still moving events, sponsors, guides, and reader participation.

What it teaches

System Role

Primary lesson
A local newsletter can build a relationship stack: lead story, useful report, community mechanic, guide/service module, and curated happenings.
Audience signal
Home/subscribe pages claim 40,000+ locals, weekenders, and wanderers.
Community signal
Breakfast Quest had 1,000+ submissions; prior burger quest had 126 submissions and top-20 testing; one edition reported 97.61 percent approval.
  • Events are present, but the product is carried by voice, local participation, and a reason to care.
  • The best lessons are community mechanics and reader trust, not rural Catskills aesthetics.
Archive + editions

Evidence References

  • https://catskillcrew.com/archive — public archive with recent editions such as MUSHROOM MADNESS, COMPOST, THE HUNT IS ON, BREAKFAST QUEST, and FIELD GUIDES.
  • https://catskillcrew.com/p/breakfast-quest — reader-nominated guide/quest mechanics.
  • https://catskillcrew.com/p/ode-to-compost and https://catskillcrew.com/p/the-hunt-is-on — examples of turning local texture into an issue, not just a list.
  • https://catskillcrew.com/submit-event and https://catskillcrew.com/partner — event and sponsor/partner intake surfaces.
Reader relationship

Ideas To Steal

  • Create occasional reader quests: best menu del dia, reliable first-date bars, best English-friendly salsa class, reader-voted brunch, coworking/cafe shortlist.
  • Keep the Edition readable: one lead package, one practical report, one community mechanic, one guide/service block, one curated happenings list.
  • Let email carry criteria and judgment; let app/web store the final guide; let Instagram show the hook and shortlist.
Taste boundary

What Not To Copy

  • Do not copy the rural/outdoorsy voice directly; Medellin needs sharper urban judgment, bilingual etiquette, safety context, and neighborhood granularity.
  • Do not over-romanticize local texture; practical trust is more important for newcomers.
  • Do not launch reader quests before the audience has enough trust or feedback volume.

Remaining Research Gaps

Before using this as a recurring system

This is now strong enough for a richer editorial-format report, but it is still not a full production research system. The most important missing layer is slide/frame-level creative analysis for Instagram carousels and Reels, especially Medellingles, Winnipeg Digest, and Naptown Scoop.

  • Medellingles: capture slide-by-slide carousel screenshots or image URLs if we want exact templates to copy.
  • Naptown Scoop: inspect actual Reel shot structure if we are serious about the Seedance still-photo-to-video workflow.
  • Catskill Crew and Winnipeg Digest: sample more newsletter issues if we want writing-pattern examples, sponsor placement examples, and recurring-block frequencies.
  • All anchors: separate newsletter-relevant lessons from SEO/lead-magnet lessons so the weekly Edition stays current and readable.

Do We Need Subagents?

  1. Now: no heavy subagents needed

    The current evidence is small enough to synthesize in-thread. I used a few source-dossier passes rather than spawning separate agents.

  2. Next expansion: yes, one per source

    If we want true chapter-length pages with screenshots, slide-by-slide extraction, and example libraries, split by anchor: Medellingles, Winnipeg Digest, Catskill Crew, Naptown Scoop, Remote Roberto, Medellin Tour Guidee.

  3. Best first subagent

    Medellingles carousel/reel forensic pass: exact slide structures, source-credit conventions, hooks, comments, and Medellin Insider templates.

  4. Best second subagent

    Naptown Scoop video-format pass: shot counts, title-card patterns, caption structure, and feasibility of generated motion from still photos.
