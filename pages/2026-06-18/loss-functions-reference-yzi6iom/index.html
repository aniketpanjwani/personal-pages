Loss Functions Reference

Prince Chapter 5 compressed into the decision rule: choose an output domain, choose a probability distribution on that domain, train by negative log-likelihood.

The Recipe

  1. Identify the target domain: real number, class label, count, direction, sequence token, or something else.
  2. Choose a probability distribution whose support matches that domain.
  3. Make the network output the distribution parameters, often through a constraint such as sigmoid, softmax, or exp.
  4. Train by minimizing -sum_i log Pr(y_i | f(x_i, phi)).
  5. At inference time, either use the full distribution or choose its highest-probability value.
Loss(phi) = - sum_i log Pr(y_i | f(x_i, phi))

LLM Bridge

A language model predicts the next token. The domain is a finite vocabulary, so the natural distribution is categorical. The transformer outputs one logit per vocabulary token; softmax turns logits into probabilities; the training loss is the negative log probability assigned to the actual next token.

next-token loss = - log Pr(correct_next_token | previous_tokens)
Research habit: when you see a new loss, ask what distributional assumption it encodes. If the answer is unclear, the loss may still work, but you should know what modeling claim you are making.
